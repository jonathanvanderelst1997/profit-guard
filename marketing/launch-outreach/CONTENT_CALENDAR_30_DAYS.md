One thing I keep seeing in Shopify catalogs:

Price data is visible.
Inventory data is visible.
But cost data is often scattered across Shopify, CSVs, supplier sheets, and someone's spreadsheet.

That creates quiet margin leaks.

Margin Sentinel is built around one simple question:
"Which variants should we fix first?"

### LinkedIn post 3
The most useful Shopify margin report is not always a huge dashboard.

Sometimes it is a short fix list:
- this SKU is losing money
- this SKU is below target margin
- this product has no cost data
- this inventory value is exposed
- this is the suggested minimum price for your target margin

That is what Margin Sentinel is trying to do.

## Week 3: proof and objection handling

### LinkedIn post
Common objection:
"We already have analytics."

That is usually true.

Margin Sentinel is not trying to replace accounting, BI, or Shopify Analytics. It focuses on the catalog layer: variant cost, target margin, supplier CSVs, and what to fix next.

Different job. Smaller surface. Faster answer.

### Case study post using demo/anonymized data
A demo catalog scan found:
- 1 loss-making variant
- 3 low-margin variants

The value was not the dashboard. It was knowing which SKU to fix first.
