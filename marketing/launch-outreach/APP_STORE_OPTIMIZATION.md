## Current angle
"Catalog margin scanner that finds margin leaks across variants."

## Suggested app details improvement
Margin Sentinel scans Shopify variants for missing costs, low margins, and loss-making products. Import supplier costs by CSV, quantify inventory risk, and export a prioritized fix list with suggested minimum margin guidance. The app is read-only and never changes prices automatically.

## Suggested feature bullets
- Scan variants for missing costs, loss-making products, and low margins.
- Import supplier costs by CSV and preview catalog impact.
- Quantify inventory risk from margin gaps and export prioritized fixes.

## Screenshot strategy
1. Dashboard: issues found, inventory risk, suggested minimum price.
2. Supplier import: CSV import, import history, updated variants.
3. What-if: supplier cost increase impact and margin risk.
4. Export/actions: downloadable fix list and next action guidance.

## Listing conversion checklist
- App Store URL works.
- Pricing is clear.
- Support link works.
- Privacy/terms/refund links work.
- Screenshots contain no disallowed pricing language outside pricing section.
- First screenshot shows value within 5 seconds.
- Feature bullets avoid guarantees.
- No beta language remains on public pages.
- App is full visibility when ready for organic discovery.

## Review strategy
Ask for honest feedback only after a real user completes a useful scan. Do not ask for a positive review and do not offer incentives.

Strategic conclusion:

Do not try to beat TrueProfit, BeProfit, Lifetimely, or Profitario as a broad "profit dashboard." They have the review volume, integrations, and trust. Margin Sentinel should own the narrow buyer-intent phrase:

> "Find missing costs and loss-making SKUs before discounts, ads, or supplier changes eat margin."

This puts us closer to a pre-accounting/catalog-ops tool than a finance dashboard. The target merchant is not asking "what was my net profit last month?" They are asking "which SKUs are wrong before I scale or run a sale?"

Ranking plan:

1. Get 3-5 real installs from targeted catalog-heavy merchants and agencies, not broad traffic.
2. Push every install to first scan within 2 minutes. Ranking will not improve if installs uninstall before value.
3. After a useful scan, ask for honest feedback first. Ask for a review only after the merchant confirms the scan found something useful.
4. Add a public demo/proof path: either a demo store link, a sample scan page, or a "free 20-SKU audit" proof page linked from the listing/site.
5. Test Shopify App Store ads only on narrow terms after tracking is verified: `missing product costs`, `COGS tracker`, `profit margin`, `SKU margin`, `low margin products`, `supplier costs`, `discount margin`.
6. Treat broad `profit tracker` and `analytics` terms as expensive unless search-term data proves they convert.

App Store copy next test:

- Current H2 is good but can be sharper for intent:
  - Current: "Find missing costs, low-margin SKUs, and margin leaks. Fix issues before they eat profit."
  - Test: "Find products losing money before you run ads, discounts, or supplier updates."
- First bullet should lead with the most concrete aha:
  - "Find missing cost variants and SKUs below your target margin."
- Add proof language outside the listing where allowed:
  - "Sample scan: 29 variants checked, 1 loss item, 3 low-margin items, $5,904 inventory risk found."
  - Do not put unsupported/guaranteed claims into the App Store listing.

Simple + technical language rule:

- Use plain merchant language first: products losing money, missing product costs, low-profit products, supplier price changes, products to fix first.
- Pair it with technical search terms: loss-making SKUs, missing COGS, low-margin SKUs, supplier cost import, margin gap, inventory risk, suggested minimum price.
- Do not hide behind jargon. Every technical term should answer "what does this mean for my products?"
- Do not remove technical terms either. Agencies, operators, accountants, and SEO searches use SKU, COGS, gross margin, unit cost, and inventory risk.

Example:

> Find products losing money, missing COGS, and low-margin SKUs, then export the fix list before changing prices.
