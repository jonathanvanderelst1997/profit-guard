# Competitor benchmark

Research date: 2026-06-13.

## Market read

Merchants do pay for profit and margin tooling, but the category splits into two different jobs:

- Full net-profit analytics: dashboards that combine orders, COGS, shipping, fees, taxes, ad spend, attribution, and P&L.
- Catalog margin guardrails: focused tools that catch missing costs, low-margin products, and loss-making SKUs.

Profit Guard should compete in the second lane first.

## Winning angle

Position Profit Guard as:

> The fastest way to find Shopify products with missing costs, negative margin, or prices below your target margin.

Avoid:

> Full profit analytics, accounting replacement, exact net profit, ad attribution, or P&L.

## Feature priorities

1. Prioritized action center: show what to fix first.
2. Suggested minimum price: convert margin math into action.
3. Supplier CSV preview: let merchants test new costs before saving.
4. Weekly alerts: keep the app useful after the first scan.
5. Vendor, collection, and tag filters: make large catalogs manageable.
6. Fresh scheduled scans: weekly jobs should fetch current Shopify data, not only email the last saved scan.
7. Import history: show when a cost was last updated and by which file.

## App Store promise

Use plain, operational language:

- "Find margin leaks before they cost you more."
- "Catch missing costs and low-margin variants in one scan."
- "Import supplier costs by SKU and see the impact before you act."
- "Export the exact fix list for your team."

## Launch thesis

Profit Guard can win early merchants if it feels more practical than a spreadsheet and less heavy than a full profit analytics suite. The product should make the first scan feel like found money: a short, prioritized list of products that need cost data, price review, or supplier negotiation.
