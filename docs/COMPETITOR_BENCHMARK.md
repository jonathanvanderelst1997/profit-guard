# Competitor and UX benchmark

Research date: 2026-06-13.

Sources checked: Shopify App Store listings, App Store screenshots/gallery alt text, pricing blocks, review counts, and Browser visual inspection for current competitor pages. Pricing, ratings, and app names can change quickly; recheck immediately before App Store submission.

## Executive read

Yes, merchants do pay for this kind of product. The market is already proven by large profit suites like [TrueProfit](https://apps.shopify.com/trueprofit), [BeProfit](https://apps.shopify.com/beprofit-profit-tracker), [GoProfit](https://apps.shopify.com/go-profit), and [Bloom](https://apps.shopify.com/bloom-analytics), plus focused margin tools like [Margin Insight](https://apps.shopify.com/margininsight), [MarginLab](https://apps.shopify.com/profit-leak-scanner), [Cost Radar](https://apps.shopify.com/landed-cost-radar), [CostIQ](https://apps.shopify.com/costiq), and [Delirious Profit](https://apps.shopify.com/profit).

The risk is not demand. The risk is being perceived as another generic profit dashboard. Margin Sentinel should win as the clearest catalog margin leak scanner: scan SKUs, show the exact products hurting gross margin, and tell the merchant what to fix first.

Critical finding: there is already a Shopify App Store app named [Profit Guard](https://apps.shopify.com/profit-guard-1), launched May 19, 2026. It focuses on order-level profit, COGS, fees, shipping, refunds, alerts, and price simulation. We should not launch publicly under the same name. The working public brand is now **Margin Sentinel**.

## What competitors look like

Broad suites look busy and powerful. Their screenshots use KPI dashboards, line charts, cost funnels, ad integrations, channel reports, attribution, and P&L language. The visual promise is "all your business numbers in one place."

Focused margin apps look simpler. They usually show product tables, cost fields, margin values, warning flags, CSV import, and alerts. Their weakness is that many screenshots feel generic: "Dashboard", "Product Cost", "Search", "Settings". They often show features but not a guided workflow.

The existing Profit Guard listing has the exact name collision and very similar generic screenshot labels. It owns "orders, fees, shipping, refunds" as the first impression. Margin Sentinel must make the first impression "SKU/catalog margin leak scanner" so merchants understand the difference immediately.

## How competitors work

Full profit suites usually connect orders, products, ad channels, shipping tools, transaction fees, taxes, custom expenses, and sometimes multi-store or LTV data. They justify higher prices by reducing spreadsheet reconciliation.

Catalog margin scanners usually read Shopify product and variant data, pull unit cost where available, add CSV or manual cost entry, compute gross margin, and flag products under a threshold. Some allow editing prices/costs. Some produce exports or weekly alerts.

Landed-cost tools add supplier freight, duty, origin country, tariff scenarios, or purchase-order workflows. This is important because the pain is not only "price minus cost"; for import-heavy merchants, landed cost changes are where margin disappears.

Order-profit tools sync recent orders and calculate per-order net profit after COGS, fees, shipping, discounts, and refunds. They are more expensive to implement accurately and create higher trust risk because edge cases are messy.

## Margin Sentinel positioning

Primary promise:

> Find Shopify products with missing costs, negative margin, or prices below your target margin before they quietly leak profit.

Do not claim:

- true net profit;
- exact P&L;
- accounting replacement;
- ad attribution;
- order-level profitability;
- automatic repricing;
- tax, shipping, fee, or refund accuracy.

Use instead:

- gross product margin;
- catalog margin scan;
- supplier cost import;
- suggested minimum price;
- read-only Shopify access;
- merchant-reviewed fix list.

## Product and UX advantages to build

Already strong:

- Action Center that tells merchants what to fix first.
- Suggested minimum price from target margin.
- CSV import preview before saving costs.
- Exportable findings list.
- Weekly alerts.
- Read-only/no automatic price changes story.
- Cost source labels across dashboard, import preview, export, and weekly alerts.
- Import history for supplier CSV trust and supportability.
- Simple pricing below heavy profit suites.

Must improve next:

1. Add vendor, collection, product type, tag, and inventory status filters.
2. Add issue detail drawer with formula, current price, cost source, target margin, suggested price, and exact next action.
3. Add "inventory dollars at risk": margin gap multiplied by available inventory when inventory quantity is available.
4. Make weekly alerts run a fresh scan before sending, not only email the last saved scan.
5. Add resolved/ignored states so merchants can manage recurring findings.
6. Add a cost-change what-if: "If supplier cost rises 8%, which SKUs fall below target?"
7. Add a sample CSV download and guided first-run checklist.
8. Add real embedded Shopify Admin screenshots for App Store submission.

Later paid expansion:

- Order-level profitability after fees, discounts, refunds, shipping, and packaging.
- Promo/discount risk simulator.
- Safe price update preview with explicit approval and undo.
- Vendor/category margin rollups.
- Slack alerts for Growth/Pro.
- Multi-store or agency dashboard.

## Visual design direction

Keep the app quiet, operational, and Shopify-native. Merchants should feel "I can act on this in two minutes", not "I need to configure a finance platform."

Use:

- first screen: Action Center plus prioritized table;
- restrained colors: red for loss, amber for low margin, blue for missing cost, green for healthy;
- compact 8px radius UI;
- dense but readable tables;
- filter bar above findings;
- clear "no automatic price changes" trust cue;
- merchant-real product examples in screenshots;
- formula transparency near suggested prices.

Avoid:

- giant abstract hero screens inside the app;
- vague AI language before the math is trusted;
- "true profit" wording;
- one-color green-only visual system;
- generic screenshot labels like "Dashboard" as the lead message;
- automatic pricing language in v1.

## Pricing thesis

The category supports paid pricing:

- Full suites commonly start around $20-$49/month and scale up because they cover P&L, ad spend, fees, and LTV.
- Focused margin/catalog tools cluster around $9-$39/month.
- Cheap older utilities exist at $5-$15/month, but often feel dated or less reliable.

Recommended launch pricing remains:

- Free: first scan / up to 100 variants / export teaser.
- Starter: $15/month for supplier CSV import, suggested prices, weekly alerts, and 5,000 variants.
- Growth: $39/month for larger catalogs, 25,000 variants, priority support, and advanced filters.

The free plan should be generous enough to create the "found money" moment, but the recurring value belongs in imports, alerts, larger catalogs, history, and team workflows.

## Belief level

I believe in the product if we keep it focused. The best version is not a mini TrueProfit. It is a faster, calmer, more trustworthy SKU margin sentinel that merchants open before supplier cost changes, promos, or catalog edits.

I would not believe in it as "another profit analytics dashboard" with broad claims and limited data sources. The name change and sharper catalog positioning make the product much more credible.
